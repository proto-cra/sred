After you submit

Once you submit a pre‑claim approval request, the CRA will:

  • contact you to review your request
  • assess if your business qualifies for PCA
  • provide you with a reference number and
  • instructions on how to proceed with applying for the pre‑claim approval process.

CRA strives respond to your request within 3 business days.

First Contact with the Research and Technology Advisor

After you have submitted a claim for the pre-approval through MYBA, you can expect to be contacted by a Research and Technology Advisor (RTA) within 3 weeks. During your first contact, the RTA will contact you by phone.

The RTA will:

  • Confirm your official language of choice
  • Explain the review process
  • Explain the SR&ED program requirements, and how a project(s) can qualify for pre-approval
  • Answer your questions about SR&ED pre-approval program requirements
  • Establish the facts concerning your claim, and identify areas they may require more information, or have questions.
  • Set-up an official meeting that will take virtually or on site at a mutually agreed upon date and time, when your staff with in-depth knowledge of the SR&ED work is available.
  • You will receive an initial letter that will summarize what was discussed during the call, as well as:
    • A meeting agenda identifying the issues and related projects to be discussed as well as an estimated amount of time (we estimate this meeting to be about 2 hours)
    • Contact information for the reviewers and their managers

Preparing for the meeting

Preparing for the meeting is important as it will facilitate the review of your claim. Below are some recommendations to help you prepare:

  • Ensure the availability and preparedness of the individuals you chose to participate in the review meeting
  • Be prepared to discuss the scientific or technological uncertainties the project is aiming to address, and the work to be carried out during the PCA period to overcome the scientific or the technological uncertainties, including how you plan to structure your systematic investigation or search.
  • Ensure you have received the invitation for the virtual meeting

Feel free to contact the RTA during your preparation if you have any questions about the review process.

Overview of the meeting

The meeting will take place in 4 weeks after a complete application is received. The meeting is to be a discussion between the reviewer and company, to provide the RTA with any details required to determine whether the project meets the definition of SR&ED.

The meeting provides the claimant with the opportunity to share the state of knowledge or technology advances that currently exists and the new knowledge or technological advances being sought through SR&ED. It also provides the opportunity to the claimant to provide details on how they plan to structure the project to ensure an evidence-bases approach is taken.

To better understand your business, a review may request a tour of your place of business or manufacturing premises. This can be done either virtually or on-site. A tour provides an opportunity for the review to visualize and contextualize your business environment.

If the tour is being conducted virtually, you can present your work in a virtual manner by:

  • Recording or photographing a product or process and sending it to the reviewers
  • Presenting slides or other documents through screen sharing during a videoconference
  • Using a camera-equipped laptop or mobile device to take the meeting participants on a tour of the facility or show a live production run

At the end of the meeting

The reviewer will verbally provide a summary of:

  • Their understanding of the STU and planed approach to address it
  • The clarification that they obtained
  • Any areas where they still have concerns and require further clarification (if applicable)
  • The preliminary review findings*
  • Recommendations for the upcoming claims

The CRA completes the review

The reviewer will communicate their decision to you by phone or videoconference, before sending them to you in writing, about 8 weeks after a competed application is received.

  • Provide decisions that confirms whether the claim will be granted pre-approval for 1-3 years
  • This response will include details as to why the decision was take, and may provide you with recommendations to help you with future claims

The CRA is committed to providing fair treatment to all SR&ED claimants.  If you disagree with the decision:

  • Talk with the reviewer(s) to try to resolve any disputes
  • Talk with the reviewer’s manager(s)

While there is no appeal process, for pre-claim approval, if you are denied pre-approval, you may:

  • re-submit a pre-approval if you make material changes to the proposed project
  • submit SR&ED claim for the project after work is completed, where you have made material changes, which could be reviewed to ensure eligibility requirements are met.
