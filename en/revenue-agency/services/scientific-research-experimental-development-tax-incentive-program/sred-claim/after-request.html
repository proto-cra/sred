Review process and processing time

Your pre-claim approval application will be reviewed by a Research and Technology Advisor (RTA). They will first contact you by phone to explain the process and set up a meeting to discuss the proposed work.

First contact with the Research and Technology Advisor

Within 2 weeks after you have submitted your application for pre-claim approval, a Research and Technology Advisor (RTA) will contact the person indicated in line 34 of the application form by phone to:

  • Confirm your official language of choice
  • Explain the pre-claim approval process
  • Explain the SR&ED program requirements and how a project can receive pre-claim approval
  • Answer your questions about SR&ED pre-claim approval requirements
  • Discuss any areas that may require more information or clarification
  • Set-up a meeting that will take virtually or on site, when your staff with in-depth knowledge of the proposed SR&ED work are available

After the phone call, you will receive a letter that will summarize what was discussed during the call and will provide:

  • An agenda for the upcoming meeting with an estimated time commitment (between 2 to 5 hours)
  • Contact information for the Research and Technology Advisor and their manager

Preparing for the meeting

Preparing for the meeting is important as it will facilitate the review of your claim. We recommend you do the following:

  • Ensure the availability and preparedness of the individuals you choose to participate in the meeting, including the technical expert from the business
  • Be prepared to discuss:
    • The existing state of knowledge or technology as pertains to your proposed projects
    • The scientific or technological uncertainty the project is aiming to address
    • The work to be carried out during the requested pre-claim approval period to overcome the scientific or the technological uncertainty
    • How you plan to structure your systematic investigation or search, including initial hypothesis and planned experiments or analyses
  • Confirm you have received the invitation for the virtual meeting (if applicable)

Overview of the meeting

The meeting will take place within 4 weeks after we receive your completed application. The meeting will be a discussion between the RTA and the business, to provide the RTA with any details required to determine whether the work in the planned project(s) meets the definition of SR&ED.

To better understand your business, the RTA may request a tour of your place of business or manufacturing premises during the meeting. This can be done either virtually or on-site. A tour provides an opportunity for the RTA to visualize and contextualize your business environment.

If the tour is being conducted virtually, you can present your work in a virtual manner by:

  • Presenting slides or other documents through screen sharing during a videoconference
  • Using a camera-equipped laptop or mobile device to take the meeting participants on a tour of the facility

At the end of the meeting

The RTA will provide a verbal summary of:

  • Their understanding of the scientific or technological uncertainty and your planned systematic investigation or search approach to address it
  • The additional information or clarifications they obtained during the meeting
  • Any areas where they still have concerns and require further clarification (if applicable)
  • Recommendations for future applications or SR&ED claims

Find out if you are approved

The RTA will communicate their decision to you by phone or videoconference, before sending it to you in writing, about 8 weeks after your completed application is received. The pre-claim approval report will:

  • Provide confirmation on whether the projects are granted pre-approval
  • Include details and a rationale on the decision, and may provide you with recommendations to help you with future applications or SR&ED claims

If your project is granted pre-claim approval, and over the course of the pre-claim approval period you make material changes to your project(s), we encourage you to reach out to the RTA or contact us.

Disagreeing with the decision

There is no appeal process for pre-claim approval.

If your pre-claim approval is denied and you do not agree with the decision, you can talk with the RTA or their manager to try to resolve any disputes.

If you make material changes to the proposed project(s), you can either:

  • Re-submit a pre-claim approval application with the changes to the proposed project(s)
  • Move forward without pre-claim approval and submit an SR&ED claim for the project after undertaking the work

Get support after you apply

Call us if you have any questions about the SR&ED program or submitting your pre-claim approval application.
